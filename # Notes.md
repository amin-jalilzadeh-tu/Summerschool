SMAA = Stochastic Multicriteria Acceptability Analysis (also written as Stochastic Multiobjective Acceptability Analysis).

It's a family of methods in Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) designed for problems where criteria weights and/or performance values are uncertain, imprecise, or partially unknown. Instead of requiring the decision-maker to supply exact weights, SMAA explores the entire space of possible weights (via Monte Carlo simulation) and computes, for each alternative, statistics such as:

Rank acceptability index — the probability an alternative occupies a given rank,
Central weight vector — the "typical" weights that make an alternative the best choice,
Confidence factor — the probability an alternative is the best given its central weights.
It was introduced by Lahdelma, Hokkanen & Salminen (1998) and is widely used in environmental, energy, and health decision problems.
